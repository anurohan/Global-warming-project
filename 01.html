Causes of Global Warming

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

The burning of fossil fuels releases large amounts of CO2 and other greenhouse gases.

Deforestation

Loss of forests reduces Earth's capacity to absorb CO2 from the atmosphere.

Industrial Activities

Manufacturing processes release various greenhouse gases including methane.

Agricultural Practices

Modern farming and livestock production contribute significantly to emissions.

Effects of Global Warming

Rising Sea Levels

Melting polar ice causes sea levels to rise, threatening coastal areas.

Extreme Weather

Increased frequency of hurricanes, droughts, and floods.

Loss of Biodiversity

Many species face extinction due to habitat changes.

Health Risks

Increased health risks from heat waves and disease spread.

Key Facts

Arctic Ice Melting

The Arctic is losing 13% of its sea ice every decade

Temperature Rise

Earth's average temperature has increased by 1.1°C since pre-industrial times

Sea Level Rise

Global sea levels have risen about 8-9 inches since 1880

CO₂ Levels

Current CO₂ levels are higher than at any point in the past 800,000 years
